Introduction

Data-driven business landscape, maintaining consistent, accurate, and accessible data is no longer a luxury but a necessity. Cloud Master Data Management has emerged as a transformative solution for enterprises aiming to achieve data accuracy, consistency, and accessibility in a cloud environment. Among the leading solutions, Verdantis Integrity stands out, offering AI-driven automation to enhance data accuracy and streamline business operations efficiently.

Cloud MDM is a system that manages, synchronizes, and governs critical business data across platforms, operating seamlessly within a cloud ecosystem. It empowers organizations to maintain a single source of truth for their master data, ensuring better decision-making and operational efficiency.

At its core, Cloud MDM centralizes and harmonizes data from disparate sources, making it accessible and actionable in real time. By eliminating data silos, organizations can extract more value from their data assets and improve overall efficiency.

In this blog post, we will look at what cloud master data management is, how it works and why it is important for organizations who want to enhance their data management abilities.

In addition, we will look at several of the benefits of cloud master data management and how it can positively impact an organization‘s overall business performance.

What is Cloud Master Data Management?

Cloud Master Data Management is a cloud-based model for enabling and maintaining one authoritative source of truth for your most important data assets. Think of the cloud as one master filing cabinet of everything your company needs to reference that has definitive value. This could be customer information or product information and specifications that an organization or stakeholder takes as authoritative.

Cloud master data management is a solution that helps organizations tackle these data management challenges by providing a centralized platform for managing, integrating and maintaining their data assets. The traditional on-premises model of implementing MDM requires some investment in infrastructure to deploy software and solutions that enable MDM, and typically have some difficulty in enabling collaboration and communications between disparate systems in disparate environments.

Cloud MDM and the capacity-building visibility and analytic capabilities of cloud computing in general can make possible the scalable, flexible, and cost-effective MDM possibilities for organizations. Cloud MDM brings clarity, integrity, and consistency to the on-premises siloed model of “master” or “customer” data. It determines the best authoritative data from the best authoritative source for data sourced from the end-user perspective in your sales system, customer data system, or the support platform for your service team.

Cloud MDM stands out due to its ability to:

  • Provide advanced analytics and reporting through seamless integrations.

  • Enable real-time data synchronization across enterprise systems.

Specific advantages of using a Cloud Data Management

There are several advantages of using a cloud-based master data management solution:

Faster Deployment

Cloud MDM solutions have a faster time to value versus an on-premise software solution because they do not involve the configuration of hardware and software on-premise. With cloud MDM solutions, organizations are able to start managing their data immediately after their subscription completed.

Flexibility

Users can access data from anywhere there is an internet connection, allowing users to be more flexible and agile in managing their data.

Improved Collaboration

Cloud MDM solutions provide a single source for users to manage the data. They provide a collaborative way for users to work across departments and allow for better integration across departments. The biggest benefit of a single source for managing data is the ability for users to access or share, in real-time, with a single source, which results in being able to make business decisions quicker and run a business more effectively.

Scalability

Cloud MDM solutions can handle more data and users than on-premise solutions and, therefore, they are inherently more scalable. Organizations can easily add or remove users and can add or reduce capacity for data storage. 

Cost-Efficiency

Cloud MDM solutions provide significant cost savings. For starters, there is less cost associated with infrastructure costs to an organization, it has no great expense for hardware or expenses for controlling the evolution of its on-premise systems. With a subscription model that is characterized by “pay as you go/resource utilization prices“, the organization simply pays for the resources it utilizes which reduce both upfront costs and ongoing maintenance costs.

Moreover, the cloud solution means the service provider is responsible for the maintenance of the cloud transparently to businesses, which reduces the IT overhead to the business because they have a service provider to ensure that no work is required by their internal IT – no hardware repairs, no software updates, or maintenance of the infrastructure.

Data Consistency and Integration

Cloud-based MDM ensures that all users and applications within an organization access a single, consistent set of master data, which improves data accuracy and reduces errors creating a single source of truth. Cloud MDM systems also can easily integrate with other cloud-based enterprise systems (e.g., ERP, CRM) and on-premise applications, improving data flow and coordination across different departments to ensure seamless integration. 

Why Businesses Are Transitioning to Cloud MDM

The shift from traditional on-premise MDM to cloud-based solutions is driven by the need for scalability, flexibility, and cost-efficiency. Unlike their on-premise counterparts, Cloud MDM solutions like Integrity provide dynamic adaptability and faster implementation, empowering businesses to respond swiftly to changing market needs.

Lower Total Cost of Ownership

Moving to cloud MDM eliminates the need for large upfront capital expenditures on hardware and reduces ongoing maintenance and upgrade costs. Cloud providers offer pay-as-you-go or subscription models, enabling businesses to pay only for the resources they use, which is far more cost-effective than maintaining on-premises systems.

Traditional MDM systems require hefty upfront costs for infrastructure like servers and storage, while cloud MDM eliminates these by managing hardware and facilities. With a subscription model, businesses only pay for what they use, avoiding large capital expenditures. Cloud MDM also reduces the need for dedicated IT staff, as the provider handles maintenance, updates, and troubleshooting, lowering salary and training expenses.


Additionally, cloud MDM offers elastic scaling, adjusting resources based on demand, ensuring businesses only pay for actual usage. It also eliminates recurring costs for system upgrades, software patches, and maintenance, as the cloud provider takes care of these tasks.

Faster Deployment and Time to Value

Cloud MDM solutions can be deployed much faster than traditional systems, often in weeks rather than months or years. This rapid implementation allows organizations to start realizing business value and making data-driven decisions sooner, avoiding the lengthy setup and configuration processes typical of on-premises MDM.

Typical implementations of MDM could last 6 months to 18 months or more, and may cause delays among multiple teams, because there are numerous systems and infrastructure to obtain and set up and configure and test.

Cloud MDM can reduce that timeframe down to weeks or months, because the cloud provider manages the infrastructure and often provides pre-configured templates to work from. Going with the cloud MDM can help organizations derive business value much faster than traditional solutions so that organizations get access to clean and consistent master data, and can start obtaining value in months instead of years.

With cloud MDM, companies can plan and execute better decisions and better customer experiences; traditional MDM delay organizations from realizing valuable outcomes, and allowing issues of data quality, compliance risk, operational inefficiencies, among other things.

Automated Maintenance and Updates

Cloud MDM vendors handle software updates, security patches, and maintenance automatically, reducing the burden on internal IT teams. This ensures that organizations always have access to the latest features and security enhancements without manual intervention. 

The MDM of the past can take 6–18 months or even longer before a system is in place due to acquiring hardware, setting up a data center, and extensive testing. With all of these elements, delays frequently happened with different teams in the organization.

MDM of today is no longer a multi–year implementation and is transformed within a cloud solution to an engagement that occurs in weeks or in months. Cloud infrastructure means that organizations can be up and running quickly, and once the hardware and infrastructure are established, cloud providers utilize templates consisting of pre-configured MDM solutions.

All of this means that organizations can achieve business value quickly (and fast!), and organizations can utilize clean and consistent master data and access business values in months, not years. Organizations, choosing cloud MDM engagements can change the trajectory of people engaged to make decisions based on data and improve customer experience; as more important than how quickly organizations can realize business value; traditional MDM implementations allow people to work too slowly, projects take longer than needed, and allows organizations to continue to expose themselves to data quality issues, data compliance issues, and operational inefficiencies

Security and Compliance Considerations

Ensuring data privacy and security is paramount for any cloud MDM implementation. Robust measures such as encryption, role-based access control, multi-factor authentication, and compliance with standards like GDPR and HIPAA are non-negotiable. Solutions like Integrity prioritize these safeguards, ensuring that businesses can trust their data is well-protected.

Data Encryption

Encryption in Transit protects the data while being transferred between your organization and the cloud service provider, or another service in the cloud, by using protocols such as TLS (Transport Layer Security) to protect it against interception from unauthorized persons trying to access it while in motion. It is best practice to mandate HTTPS and secure APIs to ensure secure communications between all entities exchanging data.

Encryption at Rest protects data stored in cloud services, in databases and file systems, and in backups. Encryption at rest is the process of encrypting the data at storage using strong algorithms such as AES-256, while the data itself can only be decrypted by an authorized user when they access it.

In addition, key management is a crucial process in security, and cloud services such as AWS have cloud-native key management services such as AWS KMS (Key Management Service) or Azure has Azure Key Vault, or businesses can choose to bring their own keys (BYOK) which provides an additional level of control.

Data Masking and Tokenization are useful to minimize exposure of sensitive data during the development life cycle, testing, and analytics, while also minimizing the exposure of sensitive data during protective phases of construct.

Access Control

With Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) users are assigned enlists based on their roles such as admin, analyst, or viewer that support and evoke the users only to access what is required thus limiting exposed data via accidental or intentional exposure.

Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) is used to create security by utilizing two or more verification factors like a password, and a code generated from a mobile device (thus requiring 2 modes of the device). This massively decreases chances of unauthorized access, even if passwords were compromised.

Lastly, by following and utilizing the Least Privilege Principle we ideally ensure that users and applications only receive permissions they require to do their jobs, and they access they only need on their job, in order to limit risk potentially associated with broader access permissions.

Vendor Security Posture

When selecting cloud providers, you want to evaluate third-party audits or certifications. Suggestions say that you look for providers with audits and certifications like SOC 2, ISO 27001, and PCI DSS. These certifications will demonstrate that they have implemented adequate controls.

One should always do due diligence with your cloud provider or any provider by asking for their audit report and looking at their compliance documentation. In addition, the Shared Responsibility Model suggests in detail that security is a collective activity ,one cannot simply hand over security to the provider.

The provider is responsible for the security of the infrastructure (the ‘cloud‘), while the consumer is responsible for the lifetime of security of their data, access, and applications. For example, AWS is responsible for maintaining full security of their physical servers and your specific permissions and encryption of the data is your responsibility.

Backup and Disaster Recovery

Automated backups are crucial for ensuring data recovery in case of accidental deletion, corruption, or ransomware attacks, as they provide regular, automated snapshots of your data.

Additionally, disaster recovery planning is essential, involving key steps such as defining recovery time objectives (RTO) and recovery point objectives (RPO), testing restore processes, and documenting procedures to ensure efficient data recovery during an emergency.

Data backup and recovery is a key component of any MDM (Mobile Device Management) solution. For example, most cloud MDM solutions have good backup and recovery options, and data is secure in case of complete hardware or software failure, a natural disaster or other unexpected problems.

Choosing the Right Cloud MDM Solution

Selecting the right Cloud MDM platform involves evaluating:

  • Scalability: Can the solution grow with your business?

  • Integration Capabilities: Does it integrate with existing systems and future technologies?

  • Data Security: Are robust measures in place to protect sensitive data?

  • Data Governance Features: Does it support role-based access control, audit trails, and regulatory compliance?

  • AI and Machine Learning Capabilities: Does it offer intelligent data classification, enrichment, and anomaly detection?

  • Ontology-Based Data Management: Can the system automatically categorize and classify complex datasets?

  • Vendor Reputation: Is the provider reliable and experienced in MDM solutions?
